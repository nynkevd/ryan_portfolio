Product Design

What is Kernel

Early 2019, Kernel started as an app that tracked the amount of days till a movie would release in theaters. I joined to do basic design work. In July 2020 I became co-owner and scrapped everything we had so far in favor of building a better app from the ground up. Meaning that Kernel becomes the central aspect of (socially) engaging with films and get a less prominent focus on theatrical releases with the inclusion of series and a place for the titles that have already released.

What I did in this project

I was the only designer on this project and orchestrated the course of development as co-owner with Nikias Molina, the founder of Kernel.

People need a complete watch history

Kernel’s original list of tracked items were only theatrical released and were removed once they released. Leaving users clueless about whether they added it and naturally wether the title already released or not.

Collections will elevate Kernel to a new level

Breaking the mold of a single list to track upcoming films currently found in Kernel which lacks clarity and transparency will increase engagement, retention and overall time spent in the app. This can be done by giving users the ability to create custom collections of titles with a “want” and “seen” collection built in and letting users move titles out of their “want” collection into “seen” on their own terms.

Everything was a collection already

Breaking it down; movies and series one wants to see form a collection of titles together, likewise for that what they have already seen. Hence Kernel will be moving forward with a prominent “add” button and icon. Wether it be to add a title to a custom collection or marking something as seen, they are all different different groups of titles. And kernel will be the first to acknowledge and optimize this.

Theather limits the experience

Kernel originally tracked movie release dates with a focus on theatrical releases however after the theatrical release titles would disappear from this list immediately. Amidst lockdowns worldwide theatrical info was of little use and users’ existing affection for series which Kernel does not offer was only amplified.

Add, remove, add

By taking the focus away from theatrical releases for movies plus adding series in the process Kernel will appeal to more of the people it’s accessible to. Additionally inventing a subtext that permanently goes below the name of a title and adapts to a users current relation to said title, users will get the information they need without even looking for it and be subliminally called to action.

Repeat to confirm

My research found that only a minimal amount of users were interested in owning a digital or physical copy and would only track this information in select situations. Nevertheless the data also showed the potential of the “adaptive subtext” I hypothesized.
